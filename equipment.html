    The HTC Vive, developed by consumer electronics company HTC and digital distribution company Valve Corporation, is a virtual reality headset that allows users to navigate a 3D space through a unique technology called "Room Scale." Like the Oculus Rift, it features two handheld wireless controllers for interacting with the virtual environment.

    Oculus Rift

    The Oculus Rift is a virtual reality headset designed and manufactured by Oculus VR, a subsidary of Facebook. It features 3D audio, both positional and rotational tracking, as well as immersive interaction with the Oculus Touch controllers.

    Oculus Rift: VR Headset for VR Ready PCs | Oculus

    Microsoft HoloLens

    Microsoft HoloLens is a mixed reality headset that runs Windows Mixed Reality. The headset is unique in that it features sensors to understand the environment its user is in and incorporate its user interface into that environment. Users can also interace with the device through its microphones and depth sensors.

    The leader in Mixed Reality Technology | HoloLens

    Samsung Gear 360

    The Samsung Gear 360 is a 360 degree camera designed by Samsung. It features two CMOS 8.4 megapixel x2 sensors and fisheye lenses to produce 4K 360 degree video at up to 24 frames per second, or can be used for 360 degree pictures. It also contains a gyroscope and accelerometer.

    Samsung Gear 360 (2017) - The Official Samsung Galaxy Site

    Vive Tracker

    The Vive Tracker is an accessory developed by consumer electronics company HTC and digital distribution company Valve Corporation for use with the HTC Vive virtual reality headset. The device can be attached to everyday objects so that they can be tracked in a virtual environment. Student developers can use the Vive Trackers to supplement their virtual reality projects.
